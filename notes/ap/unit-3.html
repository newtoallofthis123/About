LED : Light Emitting Diode

Summary:
PN Junction diode that is forward biased.

  • Amount of light is directly proportional to forward current
  • hν = Eg
    hc/λ = Eg
    λ = hc/ Eg
  • Hence, the wavelength ie color depends on Energy Gap of Semi conductor.

Construction of LED

Light is generated perpendicular to the apparatus.
The n type layer is grown on a substrate and p type is grown on it using diffusion.
P type layer is very thin to prevent loss of photons during emission.
The device is surrounded in epoxy resin to prevent loss of photons due to TIR.

Working of LED

  • The PN Junction is forward biased so e- go to p+
  • Photos are released due to the recombination of these electrons with holes. This effect is called injection electro luminicence.
  • Recombination happens in two kinds -> Direct and Indirect.

Applications

  • Watches
  • Display Boards
  • Anything with Light :)

Subtopic: Internal Photoelectric Effect

When a light of suitable wavelength is incident on an intrinsic semiconductor, electrons are excited from valence to conduction band.
It is similar to actual photoelectric effect, but this just happens inside the semi conductor and results in the generation of a electron hole pair.

Subtopic: Photoconductive Effect

If the amount of free charge carriers are increased in a semi conductor, the photo conductivity increases leading to the release of more photons

PhotoDetectors

These are used to detect light by first converting optical energy to electric energy and measuring the electric energy. These are Revere biased pn junction diodes.
Operate on the principal of internal photoelectric effect. When a photon is incident on the junction, a electron hole pair is generated.
The hole is attracted to the n type layer and the electron is attracted to the p type layer.
The electric field due to the immobile charges in the depletion layer causes the electron to move to the n type layer and the hole to move to the p type layer.

Photoconductive Mode

Device is reverse biased and left at short circuit.
Dark currents ie current due to minority carriers flows.
Increase in depletion layer width due to the generation of electron hole pair.

Photovoltaic Mode

Device is left at open circuit.
Current is generated due to the flow of electrons from n type to p type.
The diode is not biased hence no dark currents flow.
This is the mode of operation in solar cells.

Types of PhotoDetectors

PhotoDetectors are mainly of two types:

  • PIN
  • Avalanche Detector

PIN: PhotoDetector

Device consisting of a p type layer, an intrinsic layer and an n type layer. The p type layer is heavily doped and the n type layer is lightly doped.
The intrinsic layer is grown on top of the p type layer and the n type layer is grown on top of the intrinsic layer.
The intrinsic layer acts as the depletion region.
PIN PhotoDetector is reversed biased
When a photon having energy greater than or equal to band gap energy is incident on the depletion region, then electron-hole pairs are generated.
