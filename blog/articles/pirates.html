Thoughts on Web Piracy

Ishan at 2023-03-07 15:20PM UST

Okay, let's do a quick age quiz. Do you know what was napster?
Napster was a peer-to-peer file sharing service that was launched in 1999. It was the first of its kind and it was a huge hit. It was shut down in 2001 after a legal battle with the music industry. It was the first time that the music industry had to deal with piracy on such a large scale.

Torrents and P2P?

First, for the non technical people, let's talk about torrents and P2P. Peer to Peer sharing or torrenting is a way of sharing files between computers. It is a decentralized system where there is no central server. The files are shared between the computers that are connected to the network.

Wiki Commons

Now the way you can control your exclusive access to pretty much anything you offer and basically censor by using a central server. This is reduced to ashes by the P2P system. Since, in this, there is no central entity that can control the flow of data. This is why torrenting is considered illegal in many countries.

How it works for piracy

So let's say you have downloaded a movie. You have the file on your computer. Now, you want to share it with your friends. You now torrent the file to your friends. This meaning that basically, the torrenting software on your computer will connect to the torrenting software on your friends computer and share the file. Now when your friends share the file with their friends, the bits and pieces of the file are shared with your friends and your friends friends.
